## 🚀 Introduction
Dehancer for Windows is a film emulation plugin designed for colorists and editors who want a genuinely photographic result. It models how analog film reacts to light — the response curves of real film stocks, the texture of true grain, and the glow of halation and bloom — and lets you grade through a print-and-negative pipeline. Because it runs inside your color-grading or editing app, the film look is built into your grade rather than bolted on afterward.

## ✨ Highlights

### Film Emulation Core
- **Authentic film stocks** — Reproduce the color and tonal behavior of classic analog film sampled from real material.
- **Print and negative workflow** — Grade through an optical negative-to-print pipeline for a faithful film look.
- **Color accuracy** — Preserve the natural color relationships that make emulated footage read as film.

### Texture and Light
- **True film grain** — Add organic, exposure-aware grain that scales naturally across the frame.
- **Halation and bloom** — Recreate the soft highlight glow and light spill characteristic of film.

1. Open a project in your supported host app and place a clip on the timeline.
2. Add Dehancer to the clip as a node or effect.
3. Select a film profile to define the base color and contrast.
4. Refine grain, halation, and bloom, then adjust the print and negative controls.
5. Review before and after, then render your finished grade.

## ❓ Common Questions
**Is Dehancer free?**
Dehancer is a paid plugin that includes a free trial for evaluation. You can explore the full film emulation toolset in your own projects during the trial, and purchasing a license removes the trial limitations. No free complete version exists, and this project does not offer or condone any way to work around licensing.

**What is Dehancer, and what is the Pro edition?**
Dehancer is a film emulation plugin that recreates analog film — stocks, grain, halation, bloom, and print/negative color — inside grading and editing software. The Dehancer Pro plugin serves professional color grading, packaging the extended film profiles and precise controls colorists depend on for demanding projects.

**Dehancer vs FilmConvert — which should I choose?**
Both are well-regarded film-look tools, and neither is a poor choice. In the FilmConvert vs Dehancer comparison, Dehancer leans on optically accurate film emulation and a print-and-negative workflow, while FilmConvert brings its own profiles and camera-matching philosophy. Your footage, host app, and preferred grading approach should guide the decision, and testing both is the fairest path.

**Which versions of Windows are supported?**
Dehancer for Windows runs on current 64-bit Windows versions, usually Windows 10 and Windows 11, and requires a supported color-grading or editing host application.
